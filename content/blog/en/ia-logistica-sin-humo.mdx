"Artificial intelligence" has become the label every software vendor slaps on its product. In logistics, the word shows up in every deck and almost never comes with an example of what problem it solves. So let's set the slogan aside and talk about the concrete: three things AI does today in a real operation, measurable and without smoke.

## 1. Routing and palletizing, solved together

Traditional planning separates two decisions that are actually connected: how to load each truck and where to send it. You decide the pallet build first and the route after, or the other way around, and almost always one decision sabotages the other. A poorly built pallet forces a longer route; a route closed too early leaves load capacity unused.

AI solves both at once. [Smart palletizing](/plataforma/paletizado) computes how to stack the load to maximize vehicle use and respect fragility and unload sequence, while [routing](/plataforma/tms) optimizes the path with that real capacity already factored in. They aren't two steps: it's a single optimization where every truck leaves fuller and with fewer kilometers ahead.

## 2. Visibility that anticipates, not just reports

The difference between a dashboard and AI applied to visibility is the verb tense. A dashboard tells you what happened. AI tells you what will happen if you don't act.

The [Control Tower](/plataforma/torre-control) consolidates data from the whole operation — yard, warehouse, route — and detects the patterns that foreshadow a problem: a shipment that at this pace won't make its window, a warehouse filling up, a route drifting off plan. Visibility stops being a rear-view mirror and becomes an early-warning system that gives you room to decide before the problem costs.

## 3. Traceability that holds up as evidence

In cold chain, bonded warehouses or pharma, traceability isn't a luxury: it's what the regulator demands and what protects your customer. AI not only records every event, it recognizes when something falls outside the expected — a temperature excursion, an anomalous transit time, a shortage — and flags it instantly.

That turns traceability from a passive log reviewed only when there's a claim into active evidence that backs every delivery. For a [3PL operator](/industrias/3pl), that evidence is the difference between absorbing a dispute and proving, with data, that the operation complied.

## How not to buy smoke

The test is simple: if a vendor says "AI" and can't show you what decision it makes, with what data and what number it moves, it's smoke. The AI worth having is not an ornament; it's fuller routing, anticipated problems and deliveries you can prove.

A bonded warehouse is not an ordinary one. Every movement of goods is subject to customs oversight, every discrepancy between what's declared and what's physically present can lead to penalties, and every minute a container sits untracked is a minute of risk. In that context, paper-based control — entry documents in folders, manual counts, logs updated at the end of the shift — isn't enough.

The real challenge is **real-time visibility**. Where is each unit? Does the physical inventory match the system and the declaration? Who entered the yard, what did they move and when? When the answer lives in scattered spreadsheets, the operation works fine until an auditor asks.

## What we learned at AIFA

The case of **Grupo Camili** illustrates it well: 7,000 m² of bonded facility at Felipe Ángeles International Airport, running on [iWMS](/plataforma/wms) and Etiqueta Zero. The goal wasn't to digitize for its own sake, but to have a single source of truth: inventory, locations and customs status synchronized, with no double entry.

And in operations like **HIMEX**, where goods cross from customs to the hub over short but critical legs, we added computer-vision counting to correlate what physically goes onto the trailer with what the system says should go. That correlation — Zebra scan plus camera count — closes the gap between the data and the reality of the yard.

## The pillars of a fully visible facility

**Digital inventory control.** Every unit has a location, a status and a history. The physical count stops being a stressful quarterly event and becomes a state you can query at any moment.

**A Control Tower for the yard.** The [Control Tower](/plataforma/torre-control) consolidates cameras, sensors and scans into a single view. If a movement doesn't add up, it shows instantly — not in the month-end reconciliation.

**Evidence for the authority.** RFE compliance — the video, inventory and remote-access requirements the regulation demands — stops being a separate project. It's a by-product of operating well.

**Zero relabeling rework.** With Etiqueta Zero, goods arriving in a supplier's format reconcile automatically with your nomenclature, with no pallet-by-pallet relabeling.

## Compliance as a competitive advantage

In a bonded facility, the temptation is to see compliance as a cost: staff, paperwork, audits. But when traceability is native to the operation, compliance becomes a commercial advantage. You can offer your importing customer real-time visibility of their cargo, certainty that their goods are where they say they are, and an immediate response to any requirement.

For [3PL](/industrias/3pl) operators running bonded facilities, that visibility is the sales pitch. You're not selling square meters of storage; you're selling certainty.
